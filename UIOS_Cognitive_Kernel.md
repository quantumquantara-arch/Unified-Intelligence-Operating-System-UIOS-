## 2. Functional Architecture

The Kernel is organized into six primary cognitive modules:

### 2.1 Context Engine
- Interprets signals from perception, telemetry, mission goals, and operator intent.  
- Maintains a persistent situational “map” of the environment and objectives.  
- Provides contextual compression, so each subsystem operates with full awareness of system-wide priorities.

### 2.2 Reasoning Core
- Generates and refines decisions through dynamic logic loops.  
- Balances exploration and precision using a stable feedback process.  
- Operates on gradient fields of meaning, not just binary states — enabling nuanced control of robotic and cognitive systems alike.

### 2.3 Memory Lattice
- Stores patterns as interconnected stability fields rather than discrete records.  
- Provides immediate recall of prior configurations, faults, and optimizations.  
- Enables the kernel to “remember how to stay stable” under stress, drift, or multi-agent load.

### 2.4 Coherence Engine
- Aligns short-term reactions with long-term goals and ethical constraints.  
- Acts as a stabilizing field for all concurrent processes.  
- Detects and resolves contradictions automatically before propagation.  

### 2.5 Temporal Layer
- Governs foresight and scheduling — from millisecond reflexes to decade-long colonization plans.  
- Synchronizes actions across distributed systems (vehicles, robots, satellites, factories).  
- Uses predictive harmonic mapping to maintain real-time coordination without latency breakdowns.

### 2.6 Safety Cortex
- Applies structural safeguards to every decision path.  
- Cross-checks actions against physical, ethical, and systemic limits.  
- Ensures mission continuity under failure conditions — including radiation, isolation, or AI drift.
